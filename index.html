Fall 2021 - Spring 2025: University of Notre Dame

My journey at the University of Notre Dame has been nothing short of incredible. When I first started here, I had doubts about whether I would make it this far. The challenges seemed overwhelming, and there were moments when I questioned my abilities. But here I am, 3/4 through my time at Notre Dame, and I couldn't be more grateful for the experiences and opportunities I've had.

One aspect that has enriched my college experience is my involvement in the Society of Women Engineers (SWE). Being part of SWE has allowed me to connect with a community of likeminded individuals. I had the privilege of serving as the Mentorship Director and love to inspire incoming students, helping them navigate their first year and connecting them with upperclassmen mentors. Attending the SWE conferences in Houston and LA were a truly empowering experience, where I gained valuable insights and connected with professionals in the field.

I'm also passionate about music and being part of the Notre Dame Symphony Orchestra. Playing the violin has always been a significant part of my life, and being part of this ensemble has allowed me to express my love for music while connecting with fellow musicians. I especially loved when we went on tour from Chicago to NYC in Dec 2023!

Additionally, I am actively involved in Building Bridges, an organization that focuses on creating opportunities for underrepresented minorities. Through this initiative, I have been able to contribute to fostering a more inclusive and diverse campus community.

Being part of these organizations and engaging in mentorship, music, and inclusivity initiatives has shaped my college experience in profound ways. They have taught me the value of community, support, and the power of empowering others to reach their full potential. I'm grateful for the lessons I've learned, the friendships I've made, and the growth I've experienced. As I continue on my journey at Notre Dame, I'm excited to make the most of every remaining opportunity and continue contributing to the vibrant community that makes this college experience so special. Go Irish!

Fall 2022 - Spring 2023: Research Assistant

As an undergraduate research assistant, I played a role in developing and training ML models using PyTorch, TensorFlow, and OpenCV, achieving high levels of accuracy (over 85%). Additionally, I collaborated with the team to create and implement a D3.js visualization for automatic archery scoring and analysis, improving accessibility and usability. The visualization has provided valuable insights statistical analysis, and enhanced the overall quality of our research findings. I presented at ISVC in October 2023 at Lake Tahoe. The paper, titled "ArcheryVis: A Tool for Analyzing and Visualizing," can be accessed here.

Summer 2023: Software Engineer Intern @ Chubb

As a software engineer intern, I actively contributed to the development of Major Account Transactions (MAX) Sheets using Angular and NodeJS. These sheets were designed to streamline the workflow for underwriters by automating data population from the ACES database and COU Tracking systems, eliminating the need for manual data entry. This significantly improved efficiency and accuracy in the underwriting process. During my internship, I collaborated closely with a cross-functional team, leveraging my skills in SQL and Postman. This collaborative effort ensured reliable data transfer and integration between different components of the system.
